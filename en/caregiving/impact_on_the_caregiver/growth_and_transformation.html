Personal growth and transformation

The people we interviewed expressed a range of negative and positive feelings and experiences. We asked them to elaborate on the positive aspects and share any life lessons from their experiences as caregivers. In this topic page, we present some of the life lessons that caregivers shared with us, as well as the different ways that they felt appreciated. In all of these cases, caregivers presented their stories as important contributions to their own growth and transformation as better human beings.

 

Life lessons

Marc said, “Life brings you on paths and whatever happens, you will have given the best of yourself. You will be proud of what you have done for someone on this planet.”

Rowdyneko and Drew both mentioned that they had to learn to live from day to day. For Rowdyneko it was stressful for her to learn to live minute to minute. Rowdyneko said, “But there are very few situations when you’re a caregiver where you have any power whatsoever, which is probably one thing I’ve had a lot of problems with. I don’t enjoy not having control.” Drew learned to not take any day for granted but to treat each day as a new chapter. It helps him to know that his mother is in the best possible place for care.

Several caregivers learned that it is okay to take time to care for themselves. Marlyn said, “That certainly was a big realization, that I had to be responsible for not only looking after myself, but for thinking of myself and being willing to say, “No, I won’t do that,’ which is not necessarily a natural thing that most spouses would say if all along they’ve been very sort of cooperative and helpful with each other.” Marc learned something similar and said, “Taking care of oneself, this is essential. This is a second lesson. Because when you don’t take care of yourself, you falter, and you can’t help the other person.”

Several caregivers spoke about the things they had learned from caregiving. Many caregivers mentioned they had learned to let go. For example, Joseph said, “Letting go helps me a lot. It is someone else’s life, not mine and, yes I can be of service, but … I don’t have all the answers.” Being a caregiver deepened several people’s understanding of emotions and appreciation of other people’s experiences. Shayna said, “You shouldn’t judge until you’ve not only walked in their position but followed the footsteps.”

Some caregivers had to work on improving their patience.

David is working on his patience; it is a lesson that he still needs to learn.

Several caregivers spoke about learning to see things differently. Lorna said, “You’ve just got to try and think a different way.” Hélène said, “I’ve learned to forgive myself too. Like, I can’t always do everything, right? And something has to go sometimes. And as my grandson says, ‘C’est pas grave, (It doesn't matter)’ and I’ve adopted that. Because when he started doing stuff and he’d be afraid to be scolded or something, he would just ‘C’est pas grave?’ Like a question mark, but we’ve kept it now. It’s like, ‘Oh, c’est pas grave.’ And you know what? Most of the things aren’t.”

Several caregivers described how they learned to put their negative thinking aside. Others also spoke about their own growth in compassion, empathy, and serenity. Some caregivers described how it had helped them to mature or to make them stronger. Despite the positive effects, Shayna said, “I think I am a better person, but I would go back in a second. Caregiving is very difficult, very self-denying.”

“Try to be happy and positive.” Helping others is important for Madhu.

Drew said, “My mother went from having three young kids under the age of eight one day, to the next day having a severe brain injury. If I didn’t learn from that life lesson, then this would be, I would think maybe, a grand waste of an experience right?”

 

Appreciation

Many caregivers appreciated the fact that they were able to do something good for someone else, creating a sense of fulfillment, pride or happiness.

Besides having the ability to do something good for another person, several caregivers spoke about the good feeling it gave them. Daphne said that caregiving made her feel useful.

Another positive experience for some was the opportunity to meet different people through caregiving. Lillian and Michael, for example, enjoy getting to know all the young people that come to care for their son. They even host a regular dinner for all their helpers in appreciation of their contribution. Marlyn and Shoshana also speak about all the wonderful people they got to meet. Several caregivers spoke about how they had grown closer to the person they were caring for or how they felt a lot of love for their care recipient.
